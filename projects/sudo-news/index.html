Heartbleed Strikes Back

CVE-2014-0160 also known as Heartbleed is an exploit for OpenSSL affecting versions 1.0.1 through 1.0.1f. It allowed malicious actors to obtain sensitive information with zero credentials and leave behind no indication of attack.

A new vulnerability report that surfaced January 14th from an engineering company indicates that their servers were again attacked by Heartbleed while running an OpenSSL version deemed safe. Emails, passwords, and sensitive business documents were reported to be stolen in the attack.

The OpenSSL team was notified January 16th when the company realized they had been attacked. The news of the attack was leaked on January 19th and the OpenSSL team declined to comment on whether the concerned and hack were legitimate.
